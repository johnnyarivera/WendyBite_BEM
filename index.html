ABOUT ME


The foundation of who I am as a person began in the summer of 2004 in the Lower East Side of Manhattan. Playing basketball, I met friends who introduced me to an after-school program called Generation Xcel that focused on tutoring and mentoring young children and youth. At this program, I admired the vision of the program's leaders and dedicated my time to tutoring kids between the ages of 8 through 13. It was here where I learned the core values of Excellence, Creativity, Resourcefulness, Resilience and Unity and pledged to be a community leader. At Xcel, I learned about a program called Year Up and I was introduced to the Information Technology field. I went on to be accepted, interned at UBS Investment Bank, and graduated.

Since then, I have developed a passion for advancing in Technology and have continued to challenge myself. It is why I have enrolled in a 10-month Full Stack Web development program at Pursuit to advance my skills, get out of my comfort zone, and ultimately give back to my community to a new generation ready to learn how to code.

Thank you to all of my family, friends, mentors and colleagues who have shaped me along the way and allowed me to cross the opportunity divide in this country. I hope to continue paving the way for a new generation to excel and finally close the opportunity gap once and for all. Until then, I will continue working, coding, learning, and moving forward in yet Another Year Up in my life.
